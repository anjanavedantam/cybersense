About CyberSense

CyberSense is a platform built to help people of all ages understand and protect themselves from online scams and cyber threats. Cyber threats can be confusing but through games, interactive courses, and a tip bot we make learning about cybersecurity engaging and simple.

Our Purpose

Our goal is to create a safer digital world by spreading awareness about the tactics scammers use and the ways users can stay secure online by using online habits like checking sources, spotting phishing attempts, and protecting personal data. We believe that knowledge is the best defense against deception.

CyberSense Mini Course: Quick Guide To Online Scams

In today’s digital world, scams are everywhere, in emails, texts, calls, and even on trusted websites. This short course helps you understand how scammers operate, how to spot red flags, and how to stay safe online.

1. What Is a Scam?

A scam is a deceptive attempt to steal your money or information. Scammers impersonate banks, delivery companies, or even friends to make you act fast. Tip: No legitimate company will ask for your password or PIN by email or text.

2. Common Scam Types

  • Phishing Emails: Fake messages from “trusted” sources that steal your data.
  • Spear Phishing / BEC: Targeted emails that mimic real coworkers or contacts.
  • Smishing & Vishing: Texts or calls pretending to be from banks or delivery services.
  • Fake Jobs & Prizes: Offers that ask you to pay first — then vanish.
  • Impersonation & Spoofing: Websites or senders that look real but aren’t.
  • Emotional or Investment Scams: Emotional manipulation leading to loss of money or data.

3. How Scams Work

Scammers rely on trust and urgency. They fake sender details, copy brand logos, or pressure you to click a link. Always pause before you click, and double-check email addresses and URLs.

4. How to Verify Trust

  • Check the sender’s email or phone number for typos or extra characters.
  • Hover over links before clicking — does the address match the company’s real site?
  • Type the website manually instead of clicking a link.
  • When in doubt, call the organisation using an official number.

5. Who Gets Targeted (and Why)

Scammers target everyone - students, seniors, workers, and small businesses alike. They exploit human emotion, curiosity, and mistakes more than technology.

6. Why Scammers Do It

Their motive is simple: money and data. Phishing is behind most cyberattacks because it’s cheap and effective. They use fear (“your account will close!”), greed (“you’ve won a prize!”), or trust (“this looks like your boss”).

7. What You’ll Learn

  • How to recognise scams and spot red flags
  • How to verify if a message or site is legitimate
  • Safe browsing and password habits
  • How to respond if you think you’ve been scammed

You’ll also play interactive games like This or That, Myth or Fact, to test your knowledge.

8. Why This Matters

Understanding scams isn’t just about avoiding them, it’s about recognising how manipulation works. Scammers exploit emotions like fear, greed, or urgency to control how people react. When you understand their tactics, you take back that control and make safer, more confident choices online.

9. Quick Safety Habits

  • Think before you click any link or attachment.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.
  • Update your apps and devices regularly.
  • Share what you learn, awareness protects everyone.
